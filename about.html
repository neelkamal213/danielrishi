About Daniel Rishi

Founder of New Hope Onco Wellness Centre & Cannaaura Biotechnologies

Daniel Rishi coaches caretakers of cancer patients through a metabolic, cellular approach to healing — a path he began walking out of necessity, for his own mother, long before it became his life's work.

2015 — The Diagnosis That Changed Everything

When the oncologist gave her three months

Daniel's mother had lived with breast cancer for two years when it metastasized through her torso, causing pleural effusion. Admitted to the hospital with breathing difficulty, she was told she likely had three months left and was advised to begin chemotherapy again. She refused — she'd reacted badly to it in 2013 and said she'd rather face what came next on her own terms.

That refusal sent Daniel looking for alternatives. His research led him to medical cannabis. Within eight months of starting her on it, she was out of danger. Within a year and a half, she had recovered.

What Came Next — Building a Framework

One recovery isn't a protocol

Daniel evangelized cannabis after his mother's recovery, but he quickly learned that not everyone responded the way she had. That gap sent him deeper into pharmacodynamics and cannabinology, and toward a harder conclusion: no single treatment works alone. Real recovery usually needs a combination — cannabis extracts, nutritional supplements, and repurposed drugs, matched to the person.

His framework starts from a simple idea: disease begins with malfunctioning cells, and cells malfunction because of mitochondrial dysfunction — driven by nutritional deficiency, parasitic invasion, bacterial and fungal overgrowth, and a disrupted endocannabinoid system. Chemotherapy and radiation, he came to believe, often damage the very cellular regeneration systems a patient needs to recover — which is why some people come through it and so many others don't.

2018 — Featured in Patriot

A story that reached beyond one family

His mother's recovery — and the results other patients began seeing using Daniel's custom protocols — was covered by the newspaper Patriot in 2018, bringing his approach to a wider audience for the first time.
